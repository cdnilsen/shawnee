V--43 F.D. Doctored in the Sweat House
Long ago in the past when I was a child, I became sick (and) I was doctored. My mother summoned an old man. Then I was doctored by that (old man and) made to go in a sweat house twice. When he came thereafter, that's what my mother is told by him: "This boy will become well but I myself will own him. You (and your husband) must give (your boy) to me. I will not take him away. I will just be a friend to him. Probably the kind of man I am,--that's the kind of man he will be in future time. That's why we ask for him: I see him (prophetically)[:] that he will be depended on by many old men so that they may derive benefit from him because he will always keep everything which he hears. That's why I will be a friend to him: I see him (prophetically).

196. How the bark-house is used
196.1.S: hiine yeʔpiyetoowaači hiine holakeʔkwikaaneki hiini taʔpoonamowaači
196.1.G: | then | when they bring it (bundle) | then | in the bark house | that's | where they put it (bundle)
196.1.E: Then when they brought it [the bundle], then that's where they put it: in the bark house.

196.2.S: mata wiilaani yaʔleelemooθo hškweewa waaši=nitaši=maalaakwahi=paamʔθeeči
196.2.G: | (not) | let him go around, allowed | she is thought | woman | so that she may close by there go about
196.2.E: A woman is regarded [as dangerous]: so she is not allowed to go about close by there.

196.3.S: keekčitawaʔtoota hiina pehi niyaayeewa yeʔkolaʔwaapaki
196.3.G: | the one who guards it | that | alone | he always goes there | when it is early morning
196.3.E: The one who guards it, that one alone goes there when it is early morning.

196.4.S: hiini yeešilenaweewi=waači niʔki peepiyeči=pemowetoočki
196.4.G: | that's | the way they live | those | the ones who come to carry it along
196.4.E: That's the way those live who come to carry it [the bundle] along.

197. How the Claw got in the Bundle.
197.1.S: peeleko laakwa taʔpiyeči=lenaweewiwaači paaši kileki paʔta=wilawiiki kileki noʔki mata θapa wiyehi taʔwiiša=waakoteleči
197.1.G:
197.1.E: At one time as they [the Shawnee] lived there, they were apparently almost destructive; and also presumably they did not [possess] anything at the time to get knowledge.

197.2.S: hiini waači paalohi pelʔhamowaači hiini taʔwaakotamowaači
197.2.G:
197.2.E: That's why they finally unwrapped it [the bundle]; that's where [in the bundle] they found out about him [the sacred panther].

197.3.S: hiine peeleko yeešiwaapaki hiini yeeši=neewaači manetoowi mʔšipešiili
197.3.G:
197.3.E: Then once when it was morning, that's when he saw the sacred panther.

197.4.S: hina neeneewaata hine howitamaakooli waaši niʔki niišwaapitaki=kitenyeewi hileniiki waʔši=haamowetoowaači takkeʔšiwaači
197.4.G:
197.4.E: That one who saw him was then told by him [the panther] that those twenty-four men might carry it [the bundle] to the place where they camped.

197.5.S: hiine hopelʔhaanaawa
197.5.G:
197.5.E: Then they unwrapped it.

197.6.S: hiini laakwaši metaʔθoko=kiteniišwi howe weʔpiyeewa hina waʔša=wiitamaakowaači hini yeešaalooleči
197.6.G:
197.6.E: At that time twelve days from now that one will come so that they may be told by him the way in which he may be employed.

197.7.S: niičaphi hiini laakwaši mata wiʔθeniiki noʔki matayaamenooki
197.7.G:
197.7.E: Of course during that time they do not eat, and also they do not drink.

197.8.S: hine howe piyeewa hokikaloolekowaali
197.8.G:
197.8.E: Now then he comes [and] they are spoken to by him.

197.9.S: hiini yeekowaači naʔθaapi niila hiini yeelooθoya waʔši=kiteminaakweelemelako nimiilekoopi wiišikatowiiwe talaakwa taʔwaneeletama noʔki yooma nipaamʔθeewe nitapeeleta špemeki nipaapaamʔθe payeekwa halayooma wehoči=pwa=waniʔkaašiyani
197.9.G:
197.9.E: That's when they are told by him, "That's what I, too, am told, that I should take pity on you fellows; I am given an ability which I never forget [an ability to fly]; also, I control this travelling of mine; I go about above [generally] but am here now so that you will not hereafter forget me."


197.10.S: hiine hoteʔpeʔšena taʔteeki hine homanetoowiiwenwa čeeyoma nitaši hapiili hoškašeeli
197.10.G:
197.10.E: Then he touched the place where this sacred thing of theirs is: his claw stayed right there.


197.11.S: howe keela kookwalaakwaši keʔnaanaatamoole hotelahi hiini weeči=nitaši=hapiliči nili hoškašeeli hinoki naanʔθate howeši=mekinʔhwe hiina yaama škaša kehawa keʔkiikalʔškiliikweʔkola hiini wehaaši=howešilepwaaya
197.11.G:
197.11.E: "Now indeed for unknown time I will always help you," he told them, "that's why that claw stays here; now whenever you kill fine game, you must use this claw: you must scratch him [game animal killed] under his eyes so that I will feel glad [and so that the marks will keep buzzards from plucking out the eyes and poisoning the game]."

197.12.S: hiine noʔki peteki šimaataʔšinooki
197.12.G:
197.12.E: And then they again lie there.

197.13.S: yaayopi hini lekhi
197.13.G:
197.13.E: They always say as much as that [but do not always put in practise what they say].
